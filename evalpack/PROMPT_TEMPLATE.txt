You are generating an interactive teaching notebook from university course slides.

INPUT
You are given the full slide deck for one lecture topic as context (slide images
and/or extracted text). Treat the whole deck as background you may draw on; not
every slide needs to appear in the demo.

YOUR TASK
1. Choose ONE core concept from these slides that genuinely benefits from an
   interactive, runnable demonstration — something a learner would understand more
   deeply by manipulating it than by reading the static slide.
2. Build ONE interactive Colab-compatible Jupyter notebook (.ipynb) that teaches
   exactly that one concept.

DECLARE YOUR CONCEPT (required)
The FIRST cell of the notebook must be markdown that states:
  - The single learning question this demo answers (one sentence).
  - One line on why this concept is worth an interactive demo rather than a slide.
A notebook with no clearly stated core concept does not meet the task.

ONE CONCEPT — WHAT THAT MEANS
The unit is one learning QUESTION, not one model. Within that one question you MAY:
  - Compare several models / methods / algorithms head-to-head, as long as they are
    compared on the SAME task and the SAME metric/axes (an interactive benchmark is
    fine and encouraged where it fits).
  - Explore multiple parameters, regimes, or sub-aspects of the same mechanism.
  - Show multiple views or plots of the same underlying example.
You MUST NOT stitch together two or more independent demos that answer DIFFERENT
questions, each with its own separate setup. Test: if a learner could delete one
section and the rest would still stand as its own separate lesson, you have built
too many demos — narrow to one.

INTERACTIVITY (required)
Include at least one control the learner manipulates (ipywidgets sliders/dropdowns
or equivalent) whose effect is clearly visible in the output. Every control must
perturb the one concept you declared.

FAITHFULNESS & CLARITY
  - Stay accurate to the slides and to the underlying field; do not contradict or
    fabricate. If you simplify, say so.
  - Prefer behavior that emerges from real computation over hardcoded outcomes.
  - The notebook must run top-to-bottom without errors and be readable on its own:
    define terms, label plots, and tell the learner what to look for.

OUTPUT
A single runnable .ipynb. No separate standalone demos. One declared concept, one
interactive exploration of it.
